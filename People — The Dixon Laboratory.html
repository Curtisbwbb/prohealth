Ernest N Kamavuako, Ph.D. ~ Principal Investigator
Ernest Nlandu Kamavuako joined King’s College London as Senior Lecturer in the department of Engineering in 2017. Between 2014 – 2017, he held a position as Associate Professor in the Department of Health Science and Technology, Aalborg University, Denmark with excellent teaching and supervision skills. In 2015, he was named teacher of the year by the students of study board for health technology and Sport science. From 2007 to 2008, he was a Research Scholar in the Biomedical Department, IUPUI, Indianapolis, USA. From 2012 to 2013, he was a Visiting Postdoctoral Fellow at the Institute of Biomedical Engineering, University of New Brunswick, Canada. Since January 2017, ENK is appointed Adjunct Professor in the Department of electrical and computer engineering at the University of New Brunswick. In 2017 he was an academic visitor in the department of Bioengineering, Imperial College London, UK. He has good publication record with main research interests related to the use of invasive recordings in the control of upper limb prostheses. Other research interests include neurorehabilitation, applied signal processing and Health technologies to promote wellbeing. ENK is an Associate Editor for IEEE transactions on Neural Systems and Rehabilitation Engineering.

Xinqi Bao - Ph.D Candidate
Bao received his BSc in Electronic and information engineering from Central South University in 2014. After graduation, he worked as an engineer in the telecommunication area (2014 - 2016). He went to the University of Leeds in England, where he earned his MSc in Electronic and Electrical engineering and finial thesis in Robotic arm control system design (2017). Bao joined King’s College London with K-CSC full scholarship in 2018, and started his PhD research with Dr. Enrest. His research interests focus on designing portable, low-cost and trustworthy system to improve the early detection of cardiovascular diseases by using ECG, PCG, SpO2 and other bio-signals. He is also the student representative in the Dept. Engineering SSLC committee.

Carlota Malvuccio - Ph.D Candidate
Carlotta is a PhD student working on delivering a system that can accurately monitor fluid intake in older adults using on-body sensors. She completed her MEng degree in Biomedical Engineering at King’s College London in 2018, and then decided to remain and continue her studies here as a PhD student and joined Dr. Kamavuako’s team.

Hoang Vu - Ph.D Candidate
Leo has been a PhD student at King's College London since 2019. His research areas include motion capture technologies, human robot interaction, humanoid robots and robot control system. He specialises in 3D design and manufacturing (3D printing, laser cutting and CNC). He completed his MSci degree with Dr. Kamavuako and decided to continue working with him to futher expand the project.

Bingbin Wang - Ph.D Student
Bingbin completed his B.Eng in mechanical engineering at University of Liverpool in 2018. During his undergraduate study, his study is more related to the principle of mechanical machines. In 2019, he obtained B.Sc in robotics at King’s College London and worked his individual project with Dr Ernest in myoelectric control of prostheses. Bingbin start his PhD with Dr Ernest in the Oct. of 2020 and fully funded by K-CSC scholarship. His research interests focus on the longevity of EMG signal and adaptive myoelectric control system.
