Current projects fall into three major areas:

Project 1. A system for screening and early detection of heart condition
Cardiovascular disease is the leading cause of mortality which accounts 30% of death worldwide. It includes congenital heart disease (CHD) and acquired heart disease (AHD). Early screening and real-time monitoring play a vital role in the detection and for necessary action to be taken to lower the risk for worsening heart disease.This project aims to fuse multiple on-body sensors and propose a wearable system for screening and early detection of heart conditions. The specific research questions are:

  • What challenges are associated with fusing multiple sensors on a portable device?

  • To what extent fusing multiple sensors can improve the detection onset of heart conditions?

  • What is the effect of sensor fusion on the classification of different of heart conditions?

  • How do physiological murmurs differentiate from pathological murmurs from the signal characteristics point of view?
